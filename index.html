The Year 2022 marks as a Diamond Jubilee year for St. Joseph’s Convent Hr. Sec. School Sambalpur. Diamond Jubilee connotes a gracious 6 decades of instigation of this Educational Institute. The Celebration signifies recollection of the beautiful voyage of budding and blooming Josephite’s under the warmth and care of dedicated sisters, teachers and staff. Through the years the school has fulfilled its undertaking of imparting intellectual, moral and spiritual values among the josephite’s which has helped them bloom unreservedly and spread their vibrance wherever they go.

It was the year 1963, when Sisters of St. Joseph, Chambery, Nagpur Province came to Sambalpur and were approached by the Late Mr. Banmali Babu( then a social activist in Western Odisha). Their joint efforts laid the humble beginning of St. Joseph’s Convent School on 11th February 1963.

From the very modest beginning in 1963, under the able guidance of our yesteryears principals, devoted teachers and staff the school has grown into leaps and bounds, keeping pace with the ever-changing world, professionally, structurally, technically while maintaining its core values and virtues as intact as ever.

The principals of St. josephs have been the mainstay for this institute, Our present principal Sister. Anjana has continued this legacy, working tirelessly to bring more laurels to the institute. Even during the testing time of the corona pandemic under her gifted leadership and brilliant workforce the school, the staff and all josephite’s were able to sail through gracefully.

The inaugural ceremony of the diamond jubilee celebration kickstarted with the tones of bands, trumpets being played by the band squad while the Principal and Sisters received the NCC salute. The ceremony was largely conducted by the young and vivacious students of SJC who along with their guiding teachers toiled hard to make it a huge success. With the milieu, filled with hymns and prayers Sister Anjana Lighted the paschal candle, followed by Lighting of the lamp by Sister Damian, Sister Teresa, Agomany Mam, and Sir D.P. Sharma which marked the unrolling of yearlong celebration. This followed hoisting of school flag and everyone singing in unison the school anthem. The school logo was unveiled this followed a number of online and offline events by students of SJC. The events had participants ranging from tiny tots of class1 to our young adults of class10th.

The young Picasso’s from Grade 1,2,4 and 5 exhibited their artistic skills in the form of paintings honouring our Patron Saint, St. Joseph , our institution and its motto. class 6 th had competition to compose hymns on St.Joseph our Patron Saint. Grade 3 had a creative writing competition honouring the virtues of St. joseph, Grade 7th displayed their culinary skills in the competition ‘Cooking without Fire’. Grade 8th and 9th participated in the elocution competition, displaying the principles of articulation, inflection, accent and emphasis and last but not the least Grade 10th students conducted a very lively and vibrant Quiz Competition, named ‘Tarrant’. The young wizards rocked the platform exhibiting their brilliance in all the rounds. And not just this there was an interschool volleyball match organised within the school premises.

Later, a newly built cardio park and gymnasium was inaugurated by Father Petrus Kullu, Provincial of SVD, INE Province.

Friends, the ceremony came to an end but the spirit of celebration and enthusiasm continues. With the school moto “Virtue Alone Ennobles”, engraved in our minds and souls we continue our journey forward into the future always striving to be worthy carriers of the great legacy.

Thank You.
Sheetal Sakalle.
